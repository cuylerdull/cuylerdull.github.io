NORTHWESTERN FORMULA RACING
...is Northwestern University's Formula SAE team (NUFSAE). Formula SAE is an intercollegiate engineering competition created by the Society of Automotive Engineers. Each year, engineering universities from across the world design, manufacture, test, and race formula-style cars to compete in this competition. The rules and regulations set forth by SAE demand from each competing team creativity, design thinking, and engineering prowess. Read below on how I contributed to Northwestern's 17-18 vehicle.
As a member of the aerodynamics subteam, I worked on the design, manufacturing, and testing of the wings and bodywork of our car. Specifically, I worked with NUFSAE leadership to optimize our rear wing configuration with CFD software. Additionally, I manufactured many carbon fiber layups for structural members as well as aerodynamic components.
From this experience, I gained valuable engineering experience: I enhanced my SolidWorks skills, I learned new manufacturing methods, and I learned how to navigate CFD software. I additionally gained soft skills such as communication, time-management, delegation, and leadership.
